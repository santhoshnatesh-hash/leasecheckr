NJ Lease Tax Explained: How the 6.625% Sales Tax Actually Works

5 min readUpdated March 2026
Key Takeaways
  • NJ taxes your monthly lease payment at 6.625% — not the full vehicle price
  • This saves NJ drivers $1,000–$2,500+ compared to states that tax the full cap cost
  • Tax applies to both the depreciation and finance charges each month
  • Your due-at-signing amount includes first month's tax + registration fees
  • The NJ tax advantage makes leasing relatively more attractive here than in many states

One of the most common questions NJ lease shoppers ask is: "How much tax do I pay on a lease?" The answer is surprisingly favorable compared to most states — and it's one reason leasing is particularly popular in New Jersey.

New Jersey taxes your monthly lease payment at 6.625%. Not the full purchase price. Not the total of all payments. Just each individual monthly payment as you make it. This is a meaningful financial advantage that most NJ drivers don't realize they have.

How NJ lease tax is calculated

Monthly Tax = (Depreciation Charge + Finance Charge) × 6.625%

Example: ($392 + $109) × 6.625% = $33.19/month in tax
Over 36 months: $33.19 × 36 = $1,195 total tax

Compare this to buying the same vehicle outright at $37,200: you'd pay 6.625% on the full purchase price, or $2,465 in sales tax. On a lease, you pay $1,195. That's $1,270 less in tax — a real savings just from the structure of the transaction.

NJ vs. other states

Not all states tax leases the same way. Some tax the full capitalized cost upfront (like Texas, Illinois, and Arkansas). Others tax the total of all payments at signing. NJ's approach — taxing each monthly payment — is one of the most favorable for the consumer.

What gets taxed and what doesn't

Taxed monthly: The depreciation charge and the finance charge that make up your pre-tax monthly payment. NJ applies 6.625% to this combined amount each month.

Not taxed monthly: The acquisition fee, dealer doc fee, and DMV/registration fees. These are typically paid at signing as part of your due-at-signing amount. DMV fees are government charges, not subject to sales tax. The acquisition fee and doc fee are usually rolled into the cap cost, where they affect your depreciation charge (and are therefore indirectly taxed through the monthly payment).

Due at signing tax: Your first monthly payment's tax is included in the due-at-signing amount. So if your monthly payment with tax is $445, that $445 is part of your drive-off — along with registration, plates, and any other upfront fees.

NJ advantage

The monthly tax structure means you never pay tax on the residual value — only on the depreciation and financing. On a vehicle where 57% of the value is retained as residual, you're only paying tax on the 43% you're "using." This is a structural advantage of leasing in NJ.

Common tax mistakes

Paying tax on the full price at signing: Some out-of-state dealers or inexperienced F&I managers may try to collect tax on the full capitalized cost at signing. In NJ, this is incorrect for leases. If you see a large upfront tax charge, ask them to confirm the tax is being applied monthly per NJ regulations.

Forgetting tax in your payment comparison: When comparing lease quotes, make sure you're comparing payments with tax included. A quote showing "$412/mo + tax" is actually $439/mo in NJ after the 6.625% is applied. Always ask: "Is that payment including NJ sales tax?"

Confusing lease tax with purchase tax: If you decide to buy the vehicle at lease end, you'll pay NJ sales tax on the purchase price (the residual value) at that point. The monthly lease tax you've already paid doesn't count toward the purchase tax — they're separate transactions.

Verify tax is applied monthly — not upfront on the full cap cost
