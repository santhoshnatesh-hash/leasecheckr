New Jersey Tax Advantage
New Jersey is one of a small number of states that applies sales tax only to your monthly lease payment, not to the full vehicle value. In states like NY and PA, tax is calculated on the entire capitalized cost. This makes leasing in NJ approximately $1,800–$2,400 more tax-efficient over a 36-month term than the same vehicle leased across the border — a structural advantage that rarely appears in national lease comparisons.

Lease Checklist

Use this checklist before signing your next lease. Each item represents a common point of negotiation or a dealer practice worth understanding. Tick each one off as you confirm it.

Before You Visit
Research the current money factor for your target vehicle on Edmunds or a lease forum — know the base rate before you walk in
Check the residual percentage for your desired term and mileage — higher residual means lower payments
Get quotes from at least three dealers before committing to any — competition is your single most effective negotiating tool
Confirm you are visiting in March, September, or a model year-end month for this manufacturer — timing alone is worth $40–70/month
Run your target deal through LeaseCheck before visiting — know your target payment and walk-away number
At the Dealership
Ask the sales manager for the money factor in writing before discussing monthly payment — refusal to disclose is a red flag
Negotiate the capitalized cost (selling price) first, independent of any lease discussion — treat it like a cash purchase
Confirm first month only is due at signing — resist multiple security deposits or excess acquisition fees unless dealer-specific incentives justify them
Verify the money factor matches the published base rate — any markup should be quantified and challenged
Decline dealer-added products in the F&I office — paint protection, gap insurance, tire warranties — unless you have independently verified their value
Before You Sign
Photograph the MVSC-1 lease contract and upload to LeaseCheck for a final verification score before signing
Confirm the mileage allowance accurately reflects your driving — the cost of overage ($0.25/mile) compounds sharply in the final year
Verify the residual value on the contract matches what was quoted — this figure is non-negotiable once signed
Confirm NJ sales tax is applied to monthly payments only — not to the full capitalized cost — as required by NJ law
