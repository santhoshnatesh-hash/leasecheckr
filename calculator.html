How Lease Math Works

Every lease payment is two charges:
depreciation + financing.

The Lease Payment Formula
Depreciation = (Adjusted Cap Cost − Residual Value) ÷ Term
Finance Charge = (Adjusted Cap Cost + Residual Value) × Money Factor
Monthly Payment = Depreciation + Finance Charge + Tax
MSRP
Sticker Price
The manufacturer's suggested retail price. Your lease residual is based on this number — even if you negotiate the sale price down.
Cap Cost
Capitalized Cost
The negotiated sale price plus any rolled-in fees, minus your down payment and rebates. This is the number your lease is actually built on.
Residual
Residual Value
The vehicle's predicted value at lease end, expressed as a % of MSRP. Set by the manufacturer's leasing company — not the dealer. Higher = lower payment.
MF
Money Factor
The lease interest rate in decimal form. Multiply by 2,400 to get the approximate APR. Dealers receive a "buy rate" and can mark it up — always ask.
Depreciation
The Biggest Piece
The difference between what the car is worth now vs. at lease end, spread over the term. This is usually 60–70% of your monthly payment.
NJ Tax
6.625% Monthly
New Jersey taxes each monthly lease payment at 6.625%. Unlike some states, you don't pay tax on the full vehicle price — only on the payments.
